We appreciate the comment, and hope the referee agrees that optimizing

typically offer more details in the referee-response document then we put into

Before addressing the referee's individual comments, we note that we have
significantly rewritten a portion of Section 2 to address two concerns that we
believe underlie several of the referee's comments.   One is whether
a small value of the compression loss expressed by the <chi^2> quantity [given in
what is now eqn (7)] is equivalent to small biases in the inference of the
parameters q.  The other is whether the summation over samples in this equation
is a reliable representation of a full marginalization over some underlying
true prior in the nuisance space n.  The revised text attempts to:

(1) more clearly define the relation between the chi^2 metric and changes in the
parameter inference;

(2) explicitly note that we do not offer formal proofs for these two concerns;

(3) qualitatively explain why these two statements are strongly motivated
despite the absence of a proof.

> 15. Line 286: The manuscript describes some past analyses as "ad-hoc," but the
> proposed method itself appears to involve ad-hoc elements, such as minimizing
> chi-square while assuming it does not significantly affect the posterior, as
> well as some arbitrary threshold for the chi-square quantity. I suggest avoiding

The previous approaches chose a prior distribution for their n(z) functions -
what is now eqn (31) - as an intuitive guess of what variation should be
allowed.  We do offer a method to minimize a loss as a function of
compressed dimension M.  The loss function has an interpretable scale,
since it is close to log(probability), to allow informed choices in the loss vs
compression tradeoff.  The loss function is not defined by parameter inferences
directly, but we do consider this a strongly motivated method rather than ad hoc.

I am at a loss for a response here - perhaps this is because the referee and I

It is unclear to us what the referee is requesting that is not presented in
